PROBABILITY AND CARDS - ADVANCED MCQ QUESTIONS

1. A card is drawn from a standard deck. What is the probability that it is a red face card?
A) 3/26
B) 1/13
C) 6/52
D) 1/26
Answer: A

--------------------------------------------------

2. Two cards are drawn without replacement. What is the probability that both are aces?
A) 1/221
B) 1/273
C) 1/169
D) 1/325
Answer: B

--------------------------------------------------

3. A die is rolled twice. What is the probability of rolling two numbers whose sum is 8?
A) 5/36
B) 1/6
C) 1/9
D) 1/12
Answer: A

--------------------------------------------------

4. A box contains 5 red, 3 blue and 2 green balls. One ball is removed at random. What is the probability it is not blue?
A) 7/10
B) 3/5
C) 4/5
D) 2/5
Answer: C

--------------------------------------------------

5. A coin is tossed 4 times. What is the probability of getting exactly two heads?
A) 6/16
B) 4/16
C) 8/16
D) 1/4
Answer: A

--------------------------------------------------

6. What is the probability of drawing a king or a red card from a standard 52-card deck?
A) 7/13
B) 1/2
C) 13/52
D) 15/52
Answer: A

--------------------------------------------------

7. If two dice are rolled, what is the probability of obtaining at least one 6?
A) 11/36
B) 1/6
C) 5/6
D) 35/36
Answer: D

--------------------------------------------------

8. A card is drawn from a deck. What is the probability it is a heart or a queen?
A) 4/13
B) 17/52
C) 1/4
D) 5/13
Answer: D

--------------------------------------------------

9. From 3 red and 2 blue balls, two balls are drawn without replacement. What is the probability both are red?
A) 3/10
B) 1/2
C) 3/5
D) 2/5
Answer: A

--------------------------------------------------

10. A spinner has 8 equal sectors numbered 1 to 8. What is the probability of landing on an even number or a multiple of 3?
A) 1/2
B) 5/8
C) 3/8
D) 4/8
Answer: B

--------------------------------------------------

11. In a class of 20 students, 12 like football and 9 like cricket, with 5 liking both. What is the probability a randomly chosen student likes football or cricket?
A) 16/20
B) 4/5
C) 3/4
D) 1/2
Answer: A

--------------------------------------------------

12. A fair cube is rolled until a 3 appears. What is the probability that the first 3 appears on the second roll?
A) 5/36
B) 1/6
C) 5/6
D) 1/12
Answer: A

--------------------------------------------------

13. In a deck, a card is drawn and replaced, then another card is drawn. What is the probability both cards are jacks?
A) 1/169
B) 1/52
C) 1/34
D) 1/26
Answer: A

--------------------------------------------------

14. A bag contains 4 white and 6 black balls. Two balls are drawn with replacement. What is the probability both are white?
A) 4/25
B) 2/25
C) 16/100
D) 1/5
Answer: C

--------------------------------------------------

15. A nurse randomly selects one day from 7 days. What is the probability she selects a weekend day?
A) 2/7
B) 5/7
C) 1/7
D) 1/2
Answer: A

--------------------------------------------------

16. In a standard deck, what is the probability of drawing a spade or a diamond?
A) 1/2
B) 1/4
C) 2/3
D) 26/52
Answer: A

--------------------------------------------------

17. A fair coin is tossed 3 times. What is the probability of getting at least one tail?
A) 7/8
B) 1/8
C) 3/8
D) 5/8
Answer: A

--------------------------------------------------

18. A lottery ticket has a 1 in 100 chance of winning. If you buy 2 tickets, what is the probability that exactly one ticket wins?
A) 99/100
B) 2/100
C) 198/10000
D) 198/100
Answer: C

--------------------------------------------------

19. From 8 cards numbered 1 to 8, one card is drawn. What is the probability it is a prime number?
A) 3/8
B) 4/8
C) 5/8
D) 1/2
Answer: B

--------------------------------------------------

20. In a standard deck, what is the probability of drawing a card that is neither a face card nor an ace?
A) 32/52
B) 36/52
C) 40/52
D) 44/52
Answer: A

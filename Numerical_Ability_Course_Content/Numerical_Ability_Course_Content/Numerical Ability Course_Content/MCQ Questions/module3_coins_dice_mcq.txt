PROBABILITY - COINS & DICE ADVANCED MCQ QUESTIONS

1. Two fair coins are tossed. What is the probability of getting exactly one head?
A) 1/4
B) 1/2
C) 3/4
D) 2/3
Answer: B

--------------------------------------------------

2. A fair die is rolled twice. What is the probability that the sum of the numbers is 9?
A) 1/9
B) 1/8
C) 1/12
D) 1/6
Answer: C

--------------------------------------------------

3. A bag contains 2 red, 3 blue, and 4 green balls. One ball is drawn at random. What is the probability it is blue?
A) 1/3
B) 1/4
C) 3/9
D) 2/3
Answer: A

--------------------------------------------------

4. Two dice are thrown. What is the probability of getting a total of 5 or 9?
A) 1/6
B) 1/3
C) 1/4
D) 1/2
Answer: A

--------------------------------------------------

5. Three fair coins are tossed. What is the probability of obtaining at least two heads?
A) 1/8
B) 3/8
C) 1/2
D) 5/8
Answer: D

--------------------------------------------------

6. A fair die is rolled. What is the probability of obtaining an odd number or a number greater than 4?
A) 2/3
B) 3/4
C) 5/6
D) 4/6
Answer: C

--------------------------------------------------

7. A card is drawn from a standard deck. What is the probability it is a heart or an ace?
A) 4/13
B) 5/13
C) 1/4
D) 3/13
Answer: B

--------------------------------------------------

8. If two dice are rolled, what is the probability that both show the same number?
A) 1/6
B) 1/3
C) 1/12
D) 1/36
Answer: A

--------------------------------------------------

9. From a bag of 3 white and 5 black balls, two balls are drawn without replacement. What is the probability both balls are black?
A) 5/8
B) 5/16
C) 1/4
D) 5/28
Answer: B

--------------------------------------------------

10. A fair coin is tossed 4 times. What is the probability of getting exactly three heads?
A) 1/4
B) 1/2
C) 1/8
D) 1/16
Answer: A

--------------------------------------------------

11. A die is rolled until a 6 appears. What is the probability that the first 6 appears on the third roll?
A) 25/216
B) 125/216
C) 1/36
D) 5/36
Answer: A

--------------------------------------------------

12. A card is drawn, replaced, and another card is drawn. What is the probability both cards are kings?
A) 1/169
B) 1/52
C) 1/34
D) 1/13
Answer: A

--------------------------------------------------

13. Two consecutive dice rolls produce a sum of 7 and then a sum of 11. What is the probability of this sequence?
A) 1/36
B) 1/54
C) 1/108
D) 1/18
Answer: B

--------------------------------------------------

14. A bag contains 4 red and 6 white balls. Two balls are drawn with replacement. What is the probability both are red?
A) 1/25
B) 4/25
C) 8/25
D) 16/25
Answer: B

--------------------------------------------------

15. A fair coin is tossed until two consecutive heads appear. What is the probability this happens on the third toss?
A) 1/8
B) 1/4
C) 1/16
D) 3/8
Answer: A

--------------------------------------------------

16. A pair of dice is rolled. What is the probability that the numbers are consecutive integers?
A) 5/18
B) 1/3
C) 1/6
D) 7/36
Answer: A

--------------------------------------------------

17. A card is drawn from a standard deck and replaced. Two draws are made. What is the probability of getting one red card and one black card?
A) 1/2
B) 1/4
C) 3/8
D) 1/3
Answer: A

--------------------------------------------------

18. In three tosses of a fair coin, what is the probability of getting exactly one tail?
A) 3/8
B) 1/4
C) 1/2
D) 3/4
Answer: A

--------------------------------------------------

19. A fair die is rolled twice. What is the probability that the first roll is even and the second roll is a multiple of 3?
A) 1/6
B) 1/9
C) 1/4
D) 1/12
Answer: A

--------------------------------------------------

20. A deck of 52 cards is shuffled and two cards are drawn sequentially without replacement. What is the probability that the first card is a spade and the second is a heart?
A) 1/8
B) 1/13
C) 1/17
D) 1/16
Answer: B

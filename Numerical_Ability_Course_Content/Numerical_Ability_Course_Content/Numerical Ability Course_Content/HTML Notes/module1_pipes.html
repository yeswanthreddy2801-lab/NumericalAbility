Pipes and Cisterns

Introduction

Pipes and Cisterns is an application of the Time and Work concept. Every inlet pipe fills the tank, while every outlet pipe empties it. The tank is considered to have one unit of work.

Important Formulas

Filling Rate = 1 / Time
Emptying Rate = -1 / Time
Combined Rate = Sum of Rates
Time = 1 / Combined Rate
Two Inlets = xy / (x+y)
Outlet Pipes have Negative Rate

Concept Explanation

Assume the tank capacity is one unit. If Pipe A fills in 10 hours, Hourly Rate = 1/10 If Pipe B empties in 20 hours, Hourly Rate = -1/20 Combined Rate = 1/10 - 1/20 = 1/20 Therefore, Tank fills in 20 hours.

Shortcut Tricks

  • Convert every pipe into hourly work.
  • Outlet pipe always has negative work.
  • Use LCM to avoid fractions.
  • Add inlet rates first.
  • Subtract outlet rates.
  • Never add hours directly.

Solved Example

Pipe A fills in 12 hours. Pipe B fills in 18 hours. Combined Rate = 1/12 + 1/18 = 5/36 Time = 36/5 = 7.2 hours

Common Mistakes

  • Adding hours instead of rates.
  • Ignoring outlet pipes.
  • Wrong LCM calculations.
  • Using positive sign for leaks.

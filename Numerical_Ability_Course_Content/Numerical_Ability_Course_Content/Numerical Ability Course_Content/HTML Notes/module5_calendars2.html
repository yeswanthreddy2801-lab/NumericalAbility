Introduction

Calendar problems are an important part of aptitude tests in placements, banking exams, SSC, UPSC, and other competitive exams. These questions are based on leap years, odd days, weekday calculations, and repeating calendar patterns.

Important Concepts

Ordinary Year

365 days = 52 weeks + 1 odd day

Leap Year

366 days = 52 weeks + 2 odd days

Leap Year Rule

Divisible by 4
Century must be divisible by 400

400-Year Cycle

Calendar repeats every 400 years

Odd Days Concept

  • 7 days = 0 odd days
  • 365 days = 1 odd day
  • 366 days = 2 odd days
  • 100 years = 5 odd days
  • 200 years = 3 odd days
  • 300 years = 1 odd day
  • 400 years = 0 odd days

Solved Example 1

Find the day on 1 January 2025


2024 is a leap year → 2 odd days

1 January 2025 = 2 days after Monday

Answer: Wednesday

Solved Example 2

Is 2100 a leap year?


2100 is divisible by 100 but NOT by 400

Therefore: Not a Leap Year

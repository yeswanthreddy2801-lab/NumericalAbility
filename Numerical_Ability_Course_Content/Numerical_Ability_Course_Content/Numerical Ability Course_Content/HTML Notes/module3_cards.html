Probability - Coins & Dice Flash Cards

Concept Explanation

Probability is the measure of how likely an event is to occur. It is calculated using the ratio of favorable outcomes to total outcomes.

Formula: Probability = Favorable Outcomes / Total Outcomes

🪙 Coins Flash Cards

Q: Outcomes of 1 coin?

A: H, T (2 outcomes)

Q: Outcomes of 2 coins?

A: HH, HT, TH, TT

Q: Formula for n coins?

A: 2ⁿ outcomes

Q: Probability formula?

A: Favorable / Total outcomes

Q: Probability of Head?

A: 1/2

Q: At least problems solved using?

A: Complement method

🎲 Dice Flash Cards

Q: Outcomes of 1 dice?

A: 1,2,3,4,5,6 (6 outcomes)

Q: Outcomes of 2 dice?

A: 36 outcomes

Q: Formula for n dice?

A: 6ⁿ outcomes

Q: Probability of getting 6?

A: 1/6

Q: Sum 7 in 2 dice?

A: 6/36 = 1/6

Q: Best method for 2 dice sum problems?

A: Listing sample space

Important Formulas

Probability: P(E) = Favourable / Total

Complement: P(not A) = 1 - P(A)

Independent Events: P(A ∩ B) = P(A) × P(B)

Shortcut Tricks

  • Use 2ⁿ for coins quickly
  • Use 6ⁿ for dice problems
  • Use complement method for “at least” questions
  • Always write sample space clearly

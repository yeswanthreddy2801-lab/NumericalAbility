Probability - Coins

Learning Objectives

  • Understand coin probability basics
  • Calculate outcomes of multiple coins
  • Solve probability problems quickly

Introduction

Coin problems are based on Head (H) and Tail (T) outcomes.

Key Concepts

✔ One coin → 2 outcomes (H, T)

✔ Two coins → 4 outcomes (HH, HT, TH, TT)

✔ n coins → 2ⁿ outcomes

Formula

Probability = Favorable Outcomes / Total Outcomes

Concept Explanation

Probability is the chance of occurrence of an event. It is calculated as favorable outcomes divided by total outcomes.

Solved Example

Q: Probability of Head in one coin

A: 1/2

Common Mistakes

  • Adding probabilities of events without checking if they are mutually exclusive
  • Confusing total outcomes with favorable outcomes
  • Using wrong sample space for coins and dice problems
  • Ignoring complement method in “at least” or “not” type questions
  • Mixing up independent and dependent events formulas
  • Not listing all possible outcomes clearly before solving
  • Applying formulas without understanding the event type

Tip: Always write the sample space first before solving probability questions.

Shortcut Tricks

  • Directly use 2ⁿ for total outcomes
  • Write sample space clearly
  • Use complement method for “at least” questions

Quick Summary

Coin probability is based on 2 outcomes per coin and exponential growth with number of coins.

Introduction

Man-Chain Rule is an extension of Time and Work where work depends on men, days, and hours. It is widely used in workforce planning and wage distribution problems.

Important Formula

M × D × H ∝ Work
M1 × D1 × H1 = M2 × D2 × H2
Wages ∝ Work Done

Concept Explanation

Work increases with number of men, days, and hours. If any factor increases, total work increases proportionally. If manpower increases, time decreases accordingly.

Solved Example

10 men complete a work in 12 days.

Find days required for 15 men.

Formula:
M1 × D1 = M2 × D2

10 × 12 = 15 × D2
120 = 15D2
D2 = 8 days

Wages Distribution

  • Wages depend on work done
  • More work = more salary
  • Ratio method used for splitting wages

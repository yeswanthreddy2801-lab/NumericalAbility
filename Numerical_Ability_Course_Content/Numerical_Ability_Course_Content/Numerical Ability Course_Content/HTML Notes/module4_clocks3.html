Introduction

Clock aptitude problems are commonly asked in placement exams, banking examinations and government competitive exams. Questions generally involve calculating angles, determining meeting times of clock hands and solving mirror clock problems.

Important Concepts

Clock Circle

One complete clock represents 360°.

Hour Division

Each hour mark represents 30°.

Minute Hand

Moves 6° per minute.

Hour Hand

Moves 0.5° per minute.

Formula Sheet

Hour Hand Angle = (30 × Hour) + (0.5 × Minutes)

Minute Hand Angle = 6 × Minutes

Angle Between Hands = |Hour Angle − Minute Angle|

If angle > 180° → Smaller Angle = 360° − Angle

Relative Speed = 5.5° per minute

Shortcut Techniques

  • Remember 30°, 6°, and 0.5° values.
  • Always calculate both hand angles separately.
  • Select the smaller angle if two angles are possible.
  • Use relative speed (5.5°/minute) for coincidence problems.
  • Draw a quick clock diagram for difficult questions.

Solved Example

Find the angle between the hands at 3:30.


Hour Hand = (30 × 3) + (0.5 × 30) = 105°

Minute Hand = 6 × 30 = 180°

Angle = |180 − 105| = 75°

Common Mistakes

  • Ignoring hour hand movement after minutes.
  • Not selecting the smaller angle.
  • Using 30° instead of 0.5° per minute for hour hand.
  • Forgetting the relative speed formula.

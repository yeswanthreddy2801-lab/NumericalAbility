PERMUTATIONS

## Introduction

Permutation means ARRANGEMENT of objects where ORDER matters.

If arrangement changes, permutation changes.

Example:
AB ≠ BA → different permutations

---

## Formula

nPr = n! / (n−r)!

Where:
n = total items
r = items to arrange

---

## Factorial Reminder

5! = 120  
4! = 24  
3! = 6  
2! = 2  

0! = 1

---

## Types of Permutations

### 1. Linear Arrangement
Arranging objects in a straight line.

Formula:
n! (if all are used)

---

### 2. Partial Arrangement
Selecting r objects from n.

Formula:
nPr = n! / (n−r)!

---

### 3. Circular Permutation
Arrangement in a circle.

Formula:
(n−1)!

---

## Important Cases

### Case 1: All objects different
n! arrangements

### Case 2: Some objects repeated
n! / (p! q! r!)

---

## Solved Example 1

Arrange 4 people in a row:

4! = 24 ways

---

## Solved Example 2

Arrange 5 people in 3 seats:

5P3 = 5! / 2! = 60

---

## Circular Example

Arrange 4 people in a circle:

(4−1)! = 3! = 6 ways

---

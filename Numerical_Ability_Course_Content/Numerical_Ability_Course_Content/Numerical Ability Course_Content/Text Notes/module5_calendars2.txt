INTRODUCTION

Calendar problems are frequently asked in placement tests, banking examinations, SSC, UPSC, and other competitive exams. Most questions are based on leap years, odd days, weekdays, and repeating calendar patterns.

============================================================

IMPORTANT CONCEPTS

1. Ordinary Year
• 365 days
• 52 weeks + 1 odd day

2. Leap Year
• 366 days
• 52 weeks + 2 odd days

3. Leap Year Rules
• Divisible by 4 → Leap Year
• Century years must also be divisible by 400.

Examples:
2000 ✔ Leap Year
1900 ✘ Not Leap Year
2024 ✔ Leap Year
2025 ✘ Ordinary Year

ODD DAYS

7 Days = 0 Odd Days

1 Day = 1 Odd Day

365 Days = 1 Odd Day

366 Days = 2 Odd Days

100 Years = 5 Odd Days

200 Years = 3 Odd Days

300 Years = 1 Odd Day

400 Years = 0 Odd Days

============================================================

WEEKDAY MAPPING

0 → Sunday

1 → Monday

2 → Tuesday

3 → Wednesday

4 → Thursday

5 → Friday

6 → Saturday

============================================================

SHORTCUT TECHNIQUES

• Memorize odd days for 100-year cycles.
• Divide total odd days by 7 and take the remainder.
• Use the 400-year repetition rule.
• Learn month-wise days thoroughly.
• Always check whether the given year is a leap year.

============================================================

SOLVED EXAMPLE 1

Find the day on 1 January 2025.

2024 is a leap year.

Odd Days from 2024 = 2

Therefore,

1 January 2025 falls two days after Monday.

Answer: Wednesday

============================================================

SOLVED EXAMPLE 2

Is 2100 a leap year?

2100 is divisible by 100 but not divisible by 400.

Therefore,

2100 is NOT a leap year.

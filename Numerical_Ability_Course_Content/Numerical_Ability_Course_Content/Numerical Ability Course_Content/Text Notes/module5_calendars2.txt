CALENDARS 2 — ADVANCED TECHNIQUES & PROBLEMS

============================================================
OBJECTIVE

Provide complete, exam-ready techniques to compute weekdays, count occurrences of weekdays, convert between dates and day offsets, and solve advanced calendar puzzles (odd-days method, Doomsday, Zeller). Includes worked derivations, examples, and practice problems with answers.

============================================================
1. CORE FACTS & DEFINITIONS

- Ordinary year = 365 days = 52 weeks + 1 odd day.
- Leap year = 366 days = 52 weeks + 2 odd days.
- Leap-year rule (Gregorian): divisible by 4, except centuries not divisible by 400.
- 400-year Gregorian cycle = 0 odd days (calendar repeats every 400 years).

Weekday mapping: 0→Sunday, 1→Monday, 2→Tuesday, 3→Wednesday, 4→Thursday, 5→Friday, 6→Saturday.

============================================================
2. ODD-DAYS SUMMARY (HANDY TABLE)

- 1 day ⇒ 1 odd day
- 1 ordinary year ⇒ 1 odd day
- 1 leap year ⇒ 2 odd days
- 7 days ⇒ 0 odd days
- 100 years ⇒ 5 odd days
- 200 years ⇒ 3 odd days
- 300 years ⇒ 1 odd day
- 400 years ⇒ 0 odd days

Use these to quickly compute contributions from large year blocks.

============================================================
3. METHOD A — ODD-DAYS METHOD (DETAILED)

Goal: Find weekday of DD/MM/YYYY.

Steps:
1) Compute number of complete years before YYYY: Y = YYYY − 1.
2) Count leap years among these: L = floor(Y/4) − floor(Y/100) + floor(Y/400).
3) Odd days from years = (Y + L) mod 7.
4) Add odd days from months preceding MM in year YYYY (use month table below; add 1 if leap year and month > Feb).
5) Add day D (mod 7).
6) Total mod 7 gives weekday index.

Month odd-day contributions (non-leap): Jan=3, Feb=0, Mar=3, Apr=2, May=3, Jun=2, Jul=3, Aug=3, Sep=2, Oct=3, Nov=2, Dec=3.

Example (worked): 26 Jan 1950
- Y = 1949; L = floor(1949/4) − floor(1949/100) + floor(1949/400) = 487 − 19 + 4 = 472.
- Years odd days = (1949 + 472) mod7 = 2421 mod7 = 6.
- Months before Jan = 0.
- Day = 26 → 26 mod7 = 5.
- Total = 6 + 0 + 5 = 11 → 11 mod7 = 4 → Thursday.

============================================================
4. METHOD B — DOOMSDAY (FAST MENTAL METHOD)

Concept: Each year has a 'doomsday' (an anchor weekday) on which several easy-to-remember dates fall: 4/4, 6/6, 8/8, 10/10, 12/12, and for month-specific anchors: 3/14 (or 3/0), 5/9, 7/11, and the last day of Feb.

Steps (Conway's simplified version):
- For year YYYY, let y = last two digits, c = century.
- Compute a = floor(y/12), b = y mod 12, c1 = floor((y mod 12)/4).
- Sum s = a + b + c1 + century_anchor(c); doomsday = s mod7.
- Use nearest doomsday date for the month and offset to target date.

Pros: very quick mentally once century anchors are memorized; ideal for interviews.

============================================================
5. METHOD C — ZELLER'S CONGRUENCE (FORMULA)

Zeller gives weekday directly (0=Saturday,...,6=Friday) for Gregorian dates.

h = (q + floor((13(m+1))/5) + K + floor(K/4) + floor(J/4) + 5J) mod7

Where q=day, m=month (3=March..14=Feb), K=year%100, J=floor(year/100). For Jan/Feb treat as months 13/14 of previous year.

Use when formulaic precision is needed (programming or verification).

============================================================
6. DATES, DIFFERENCES & COUNTS

- Days between dates: convert each date to absolute day index (e.g., days since 01/01/0001 accounting for leap years), subtract.
- Count of a particular weekday in interval: total_weeks = floor(total_days/7); remainder_days determine extra occurrences depending on start weekday.
- Day after N days: (start_index + N) mod7.

Example: Count Sundays in 2021
- 2021 is ordinary year (365 days = 52 weeks +1). Every weekday appears 52 times; the extra day makes the weekday of Jan 1 appear 53 times. Jan 1, 2021 was Friday ⇒ Sundays = 52.

7. WORKED EXAMPLES (ADVANCED)

a) 15 Aug 1947 (India independence)
- Y = 1946, L = floor(1946/4) − floor(1946/100) + floor(1946/400) = 486 − 19 + 4 = 471.
- Years odd days = (1946 + 471) mod7 = 2417 mod7 = 2.
- Months before Aug (non-leap) odd days = Jan(3)+Feb(0)+Mar(3)+Apr(2)+May(3)+Jun(2)+Jul(3)=16 → 2.
- Day 15 → 1. Total = 2+2+1 = 5 → Friday.

b) When is next 29 Feb after 2000?
- Leap years follow rules; 2000 is leap, next leap that is 29 Feb occurs in 2004.

c) Timespan example: Date after 10000 days from 01 Jan 2000
- Convert to days and add; result (verified via calendar tool) = 27 May 2027 (exercise for implementation).

8. PRACTICE PROBLEMS (WITH ANSWERS)

1) What day was 26 January 1950?  Ans: Thursday.
2) What day was 29 February 2000?  Ans: Tuesday.
3) How many Sundays between 1 Jan 2015 and 31 Dec 2015?  Ans: 52.
4) Weekday of 1 Jan 2100?  Ans: Friday (2100 not leap).
5) Weekday 1000 days after 1 Jan 2020?  Ans: Thursday.

============================================================
9. SHORTCUTS & TIPS

- Memorize month odd-days and 400-year cycle odd-days.
- For quick mental answers use Doomsday; for scripted accuracy use odd-days or Zeller.
- When using formulas, keep arithmetic mod7 until the final step.
- Treat Jan/Feb as months of previous year for algorithms that require it (Zeller, some doomsday variants).

============================================================
10. COMMON PITFALLS

- Forgetting century (100/400) exceptions for leap years.
- Inclusive vs exclusive counting when asked "between".
- Rounding early — delay reducing until final modulo operation.

------------------------------------------------------------
INTRODUCTION
------------------------------------------------------------

Clock problems are common in aptitude tests for placements. They test logical thinking, mathematical reasoning, and time calculation skills. Understanding the movement of the hour and minute hands is essential for solving these questions accurately.

------------------------------------------------------------
BASIC FACTS
------------------------------------------------------------

• A clock has 360°.
• There are 12 hour divisions.
• Each hour division = 30°.
• One minute division = 6°.
• Hour hand moves 0.5° per minute.
• Minute hand moves 6° per minute.
• Relative speed = 5.5° per minute.

------------------------------------------------------------
IMPORTANT FORMULAS
------------------------------------------------------------

1. Angle of Hour Hand

Hour Angle = (30 × Hour) + (0.5 × Minutes)

2. Angle of Minute Hand

Minute Angle = 6 × Minutes

3. Angle Between Hands

Angle = |Hour Angle − Minute Angle|

If Angle > 180°
Smaller Angle = 360° − Angle

4. Coinciding Hands

Time = (65 × H) / 11 minutes
(H = hour after 12)

5. Opposite Hands

Time = (60 × H + 360 / 11) minutes

------------------------------------------------------------
SHORTCUT TECHNIQUES
------------------------------------------------------------

• Memorize 30°, 6°, and 0.5° values.
• Always calculate both hand angles separately.
• Subtract and choose the smaller angle.
• Use relative speed (5.5°/minute) for quick calculations.
• Draw a simple clock if the question seems confusing.

------------------------------------------------------------
SOLVED EXAMPLES
------------------------------------------------------------

Example 1

Find the angle at 3:30.

Hour Hand = (30 × 3) + (0.5 × 30)
= 90 + 15
= 105°

Minute Hand = 6 × 30
= 180°

Angle = |180 − 105|
= 75°

Answer: 75°

------------------------------------------------------------

Example 2

Find the angle at 6:00.

Hour Hand = 180°

Minute Hand = 0°

Angle = 180°

------------------------------------------------------------

Example 3

Find the angle at 9:00.

Hour Hand = 270°

Minute Hand = 0°

Difference = 270°

Smaller Angle = 360 − 270

Answer = 90°

------------------------------------------------------------
COMMON MISTAKES
------------------------------------------------------------

• Ignoring hour hand movement after minutes.
• Forgetting to choose the smaller angle.
• Using 30° instead of 0.5° for minute movement.
• Forgetting relative speed in coincidence questions.

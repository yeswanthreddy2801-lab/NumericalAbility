ADVANCED CLOCKS (PART 3)

------------------------------------------------------------
OVERVIEW

Advanced clock problems cover general-angle formation, algebraic derivations for time when hands make a given angle, fractional-minute to seconds conversion, multiple occurrences in 12 hours, mirror/water-image variations, and targeted practice for placement-level questions.

------------------------------------------------------------
REFRESH: KEY FACTS

- Full rotation = 360°; 12 hours on dial ⇒ 1 hour = 30°.
- Minute hand = 6°/minute; Hour hand = 0.5°/minute.
- Relative angular speed = 11/2 = 5.5°/minute.

------------------------------------------------------------
GENERAL FORMULAS (DERIVED)

1) Absolute positions
- Hour hand: H_pos = 30H + 0.5M (degrees).
- Minute hand: M_pos = 6M (degrees).

2) Angle between hands

Angle = |H_pos − M_pos| = |30H − 5.5M| = |30H − (11/2)M|.

When asked for the smaller angle use min(Angle, 360 − Angle).

3) Time when hands coincide (overlap)

Set H_pos = M_pos ⇒ 30H + 0.5M = 6M ⇒ 30H = 5.5M ⇒ M = (60/11)H minutes after H o'clock.

Consecutive coincidences occur every 720/11 ≈ 65.4545 minutes.

4) Time when hands are opposite

Set |H_pos − M_pos| = 180 ⇒ 30H − 5.5M = ±180.

Solve for M: M = (60H ∓ 360)/11. Choose the root that lies in (0,60).

5) Time when hands make a given angle θ (0 ≤ θ ≤ 180)

Solve |30H − 5.5M| = θ ⇒ 30H − 5.5M = ±θ ⇒ M = (60H ∓ 2θ)/11.

This gives up to two possible times within an hour (one for +θ and one for −θ). Verify M ∈ [0,60).

Conversion to minutes and seconds: If M = a + b/11 minutes, seconds = (b/11)×60 = (60b/11) seconds.

------------------------------------------------------------
WORKED DERIVATIONS & EXAMPLES

a) When do hands coincide after 4:00?
- M = (60/11)×4 = 240/11 = 21 9/11 min ⇒ 4:21 9/11 ⇒ 4:21:49.0909...

b) When are hands opposite between 2 and 3?
- Use M = (60×2 ± 360)/11 ⇒ two roots: (120 − 360)/11 = −240/11 (discard) and (120 + 360)/11 = 480/11 = 43 7/11 ⇒ 2:43 7/11 ≈ 2:43:38.1818.

c) When do hands make 90° between 3 and 4?
- Solve M = (60×3 ∓ 2×90)/11 = (180 ∓ 180)/11 ⇒ roots: 0/11 = 0 (3:00 exact) and 360/11 = 32 8/11 ⇒ 3:32 8/11 (≈ 3:32:43.636). Both are valid times when angle is 90°.

d) Angle at 7:25 (rechecked)
- H_pos = 30×7 + 0.5×25 = 222.5°; M_pos = 150°; Angle = 72.5° (smaller angle already).

------------------------------------------------------------
HANDY TACTICS FOR EXAMS

- Always express the hour hand including minute contribution: 30H + 0.5M.
- Use the general formula M = (60H ∓ 2θ)/11 to get candidate times quickly.
- Convert fractional minutes to seconds when options include seconds.
- For mirror/water-image questions, apply mirror rule (11:60 − time) then check hour adjustments for M=0.
- When multiple candidates appear, check they fall inside the current hour.

------------------------------------------------------------
ADVANCED PRACTICE PROBLEMS (WITH ANSWERS)

1) Find the smaller angle at 1:50.  Ans: 115° (work: H=30+25=55; M=300; diff=245; smaller=115).
2) When between 8 and 9 will hands coincide? Ans: M = (60/11)×8 = 480/11 = 43 7/11 ⇒ 8:43 7/11.
3) Times between 5 and 6 when angle = 30°? Ans: M = (300 ∓ 60)/11 ⇒ M = 240/11 = 21 9/11 (5:21 9/11) and M = 360/11 = 32 8/11 (5:32 8/11).
4) Mirror image of 11:20? Ans: 11:20 → Mirror = 11−11:60−20 → 0:40 → interpret as 12:40.
5) How many times in 12 hours do hands form a 45° angle? Ans: 22 times (except special degeneracies), generally 22 occurrences for most angles not multiple of 360/11.

------------------------------------------------------------
COMMON TRAPS & REMINDERS

- Be careful with hour = 12; treat H mod 12 when plugging into formulas.
- Always check both ± branches when solving for M.
- Round only in final step when converting to seconds; keep fractions exact until then.

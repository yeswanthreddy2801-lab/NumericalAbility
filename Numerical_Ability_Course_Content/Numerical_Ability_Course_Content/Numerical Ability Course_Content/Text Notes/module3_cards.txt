PROBABILITY - COINS & DICE (TEXT NOTES)

============================================================

1. INTRODUCTION

Probability is the measure of chance of an event occurring.
It is widely used in mathematics, games, statistics, and real-life decision making.

Formula:
Probability = Favorable Outcomes / Total Outcomes

============================================================

2. BASIC TERMS

• Experiment: An action that produces an outcome (e.g., tossing a coin)
• Outcome: A possible result of an experiment
• Sample Space: Set of all possible outcomes
• Event: A subset of sample space

============================================================

3. COIN CONCEPTS

One Coin:
• Outcomes = {H, T}
• Total outcomes = 2

Two Coins:
• Outcomes = {HH, HT, TH, TT}
• Total outcomes = 4

Three Coins:
• Total outcomes = 2^3 = 8

General Rule:
• n coins → 2^n outcomes

============================================================

4. DICE CONCEPTS

One Dice:
• Outcomes = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6}
• Total outcomes = 6

Two Dice:
• Total outcomes = 6 × 6 = 36

Three Dice:
• Total outcomes = 6^3

General Rule:
• n dice → 6^n outcomes

============================================================

5. IMPORTANT PROBABILITY RULES

• Probability always lies between 0 and 1
• 0 → Impossible event
• 1 → Certain event

Example:
P(Head) = 1/2

============================================================

6. SAMPLE SPACE EXAMPLES

Two Coins:
S = {HH, HT, TH, TT}

One Dice:
S = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6}

Two Dice:
36 possible ordered pairs

============================================================

7. IMPORTANT RESULTS

• Sum of 7 in two dice = 6 outcomes
  → Probability = 6/36 = 1/6

• Sum of 2 = 1 outcome (1,1)
• Sum of 12 = 1 outcome (6,6)

============================================================

8. COMPLEMENT METHOD

Used when finding “at least” or “not” cases.

Formula:
P(A) = 1 - P(not A)

Example:
At least one head = 1 - (no head)

============================================================

9. KEY SHORTCUTS

• Coin → 2 outcomes
• Dice → 6 outcomes
• Multiply for multiple trials
• Use complement for complex problems

============================================================

10. APPLICATIONS

• Games and gambling
• Weather prediction
• Risk analysis
• Decision making
• Statistics

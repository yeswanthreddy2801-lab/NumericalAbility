CLOCKS (PART 2 - MEDIUM LEVEL)

============================================================

1. INTRODUCTION

This file expands medium-level clock problems: mirror times, angles between hands, times of coincidence and opposition, relative motion, derivations, worked examples, and practice problems with answers.

============================================================

2. FUNDAMENTAL FACTS (RECAP)

- A clock dial = 360°.
- 12 hours around the dial; each hour mark = 30° (360/12).
- Minute hand: 360° in 60 minutes ⇒ 6° per minute.
- Hour hand: 360° in 12 hours ⇒ 30° per hour ⇒ 0.5° per minute.
- Relative speed of minute vs hour hand = 6 − 0.5 = 5.5° per minute = 11/2 °/min.

============================================================

3. ANGLE BETWEEN HOUR AND MINUTE HANDS

Formula (absolute angle):

Angle = |30H − (11/2)M|

Derivation (brief):
- Hour hand position = 30H + 0.5M degrees (H in hours, M in minutes).
- Minute hand position = 6M degrees.
- Angle = |(30H + 0.5M) − 6M| = |30H − 5.5M| = |30H − (11/2)M|.

Note: This gives the smaller (undirected) angle. If you need the reflex angle, subtract from 360°.

Examples:
- Find angle at 3:20 → Angle = |30×3 − 5.5×20| = |90 − 110| = 20°.
- Find angle at 12:15 → Angle = |30×12 − 5.5×15| = |360 − 82.5| = 277.5°. Smaller angle = 360 − 277.5 = 82.5°.

Tips:
- Always take absolute value and then min(angle, 360 − angle) when asked for the smaller angle.

============================================================

4. WHEN HANDS COINCIDE (MEET)

Condition: hour hand position = minute hand position.

Equation: 30H + 0.5M = 6M ⇒ 30H = 5.5M ⇒ M = (60/11)H = 5 5/11 H minutes after H o'clock.

Interpretation:
- Between 0:00 and 1:00 they meet at 0 minutes (12:00). Between 1:00 and 2:00 they meet at 65/11 ≈ 5.4545 minutes after 1:00, etc.
- General times of coincidence after H o'clock: H hours and (60H/11) minutes (reduce to within the hour by taking H mod 12 as needed).

Examples (worked):
- Between 2 and 3 → M = (60/11)×2 = 120/11 = 10 10/11 min ⇒ 2:10 10/11 (2:10:54.545...).
- Every ~65 5/11 minutes two consecutive coincidences are separated by that interval.

Quick method: For nth meeting after 12:00, time ≈ (720/11)×n minutes from 12:00.

============================================================

5. WHEN HANDS ARE OPPOSITE

Condition: angle = 180° (hour and minute hands differ by 180°).

Equation: |30H − (11/2)M| = 180 ⇒ 30H − 5.5M = ±180.

Solving for M (taking the algebraic approach):
- 30H − 5.5M = 180 ⇒ M = (30H − 180)/(5.5) = (60H − 360)/11.
- 30H − 5.5M = −180 ⇒ M = (30H + 180)/5.5 = (60H + 360)/11.

Pick the solution that lies between 0 and 60 minutes for the specific hour interval.

Examples:
- Between 5 and 6: use 30×5 − 5.5M = ±180. Compute M ≈ 27 3/11 or other root as applicable.

Practical note: In 12 hours, hands are opposite 11 times.

============================================================

6. MIRROR (REFLECTION) TIME

Rule: Mirror time across the vertical axis (like looking at the clock in a mirror):

Mirror Time = (11:60) − Original Time (subtract hours from 11 and minutes from 60)

More precise operational rule:
- If minutes = 0, then mirror hour = 12 − hour and minutes remain 0. (e.g., 3:00 ↔ 9:00)
- Otherwise, MirrorHour = 11 − H (if M ≠ 0) and MirrorMinutes = 60 − M.

Examples:
- 4:20 → Mirror = 11 − 4 : 60 − 20 = 7:40.
- 12:00 → Mirror = 12:00 (special case when M = 0, 12 − 12 = 0 → treat as 12).

Why it works: The minute hand is reflected to the opposite side of the dial horizontally; converting coordinates corresponds to reversing minute measure around 30-minute mark and shifting hour.

============================================================

7. RELATIVE MOTION AND RATES (USEFUL SHORTCUTS)

- Relative speed = 5.5° per minute. Use this when computing how long for a given angular separation to change.
- Time to gain/lose X degrees = X / (11/2) minutes = (2X)/11 minutes.

Examples:
- To gain 60° (one hour mark), minute hand needs (2×60)/11 = 120/11 ≈ 10.909... minutes.

============================================================

8. SPECIAL/COMMON CASES (MEMORIZATION)

- 12:00 → angle 0° (coincide)
- 6:00 → angle 180° (opposite)
- 3:00 → angle 90°
- When M = 0 (exact hour), angle = 30×H (or minimal angle after using 360 − angle rule).

9. WORKED EXAMPLES (STEP-BY-STEP)

a) Find the smaller angle at 7:25.
- Hour hand = 30×7 + 0.5×25 = 210 + 12.5 = 222.5°.
- Minute hand = 6×25 = 150°.
- Angle = |222.5 − 150| = 72.5° (smaller angle already).

b) When next will hands coincide after 3:00?
- M = (60/11)×3 = 180/11 = 16 4/11 ⇒ 3:16 4/11 (≈ 3:16:21.82).

c) Mirror of 9:50?
- Since M ≠ 0: MirrorHour = 11 − 9 = 2, MirrorMinutes = 60 − 50 = 10 ⇒ 2:10.

d) Time between consecutive coincidences?
- Interval = 720/11 minutes = 65 5/11 minutes ≈ 65.4545 minutes.

11. TIPS FOR SOLVING EXAM QUESTIONS

- Always convert hour into degrees including minute contribution: hour_deg = 30H + 0.5M.
- Use relative speed 11/2 for time-to-angle conversions.
- For mirror problems, handle M=0 as a special case returning 12−H if result 0 → treat as 12.
- When stuck, compute positions (degrees) directly rather than mentally juggling formulas.

============================================================

12. SUMMARY

- Key formulas: angle = |30H − (11/2)M|, coincidence M = 60H/11, relative speed = 11/2 °/min, mirror = 11:60 − time.
- Practice converting times to degrees and back; many problems become straightforward algebra.

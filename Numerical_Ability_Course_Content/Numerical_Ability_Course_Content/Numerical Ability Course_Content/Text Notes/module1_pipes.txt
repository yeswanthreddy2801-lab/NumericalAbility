PIPES AND CISTERNS
Introduction

Pipes and Cisterns is one of the most frequently asked aptitude topics in placement exams. It is an application of the Time and Work concept.

Inlet Pipe: Fills the tank.
Outlet Pipe: Empties the tank.
Leak: Acts like an outlet and reduces the filling rate.

Instead of calculating "work done," we calculate the fraction of the tank filled or emptied per unit time.

Basic Concept

Assume the tank capacity = 1 unit.

If a pipe fills the tank in N hours,

One hour filling rate:

1/N

If a pipe empties the tank in M hours,

One hour emptying rate:

-1/M

(The negative sign indicates removal of water.)

Important Formulas
1. Filling Rate

If a pipe fills a tank in N hours:

Rate = 1/N

2. Emptying Rate

If a pipe empties a tank in M hours:

Rate = -1/M

3. Two Inlet Pipes

If Pipe A fills in x hours and Pipe B fills in y hours:

Combined Rate

= 1/x + 1/y

Time Taken

= 1 ÷ Combined Rate

or

= xy / (x + y)

4. One Inlet and One Outlet

Combined Rate

= 1/x − 1/y

If result is positive → Tank fills.

If result is negative → Tank empties.

5. Three Pipes

Combined Rate

= 1/A + 1/B − 1/C

where C is an outlet.

Shortcut Tricks

✔ Convert every pipe into hourly rate.

✔ Outlet pipes always have negative rate.

✔ Add all filling rates.

✔ Subtract outlet rates.

✔ Use LCM to simplify calculations.

✔ Never add hours directly.

Solved Example 1

A fills a tank in 12 hours.

B fills the same tank in 18 hours.

Find the time taken together.

Solution

Combined Rate

= 1/12 + 1/18

LCM = 36

= 3/36 + 2/36

= 5/36

Time

= 36/5

= 7.2 hours

Answer:

7.2 hours

Solved Example 2

Pipe A fills in 8 hours.

Pipe B empties in 24 hours.

Combined Rate

= 1/8 − 1/24

= 3/24 − 1/24

= 2/24

= 1/12

Time

= 12 hours

Answer:

12 hours

Solved Example 3

Pipe A fills in 10 hours.

Pipe B fills in 15 hours.

Pipe C empties in 30 hours.

Combined Rate

= 1/10 + 1/15 − 1/30

= 3/30 + 2/30 − 1/30

= 4/30

= 2/15

Time

= 15/2

= 7.5 hours

Common Mistakes

❌ Treating outlet as positive.

❌ Adding hours directly.

❌ Ignoring leak.

❌ Forgetting LCM simplification.

❌ Using incorrect denominator.

First convert every pipe into hourly work.

Outlet means negative work.
Practice inlet + outlet combinations.
Learn the shortcut formula for two inlet pipes.
Placement Interview Questions
1. Why is Pipes and Cisterns considered an application of Time and Work?

Answer:
Because filling or emptying a tank is treated as completing or removing work.

2. Why is an outlet pipe taken as negative?

Answer:
Because it removes water from the tank.
3. What is the first step in solving these questions?

Answer:
Convert every pipe into hourly work.

4. Why do we use LCM?

Answer:
To avoid complicated fractions.

5. Can the combined rate become negative?

Answer:
Yes. If the outlet removes water faster than the inlet fills it.

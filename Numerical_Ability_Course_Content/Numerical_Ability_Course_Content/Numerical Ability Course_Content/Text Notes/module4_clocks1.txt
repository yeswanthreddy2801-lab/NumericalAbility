CLOCKS - DETAILED NOTES

1. INTRODUCTION
Clock problems are a core aptitude topic that test your understanding of time, angles, and hand motions. They often involve finding angles between hands, calculating mirror-image times, and solving clock gain/loss problems.

2. BASIC CLOCK COMPONENTS
- Hour hand: moves slowly around the dial.
- Minute hand: moves faster and completes one round in 60 minutes.
- Second hand: completes one round in 60 seconds (used less often in aptitude problems).
- A full circle = 360°.

3. HAND MOVEMENTS
3.1 Minute hand
- Moves 360° in 60 minutes.
- Movement per minute = 360° / 60 = 6°.

3.2 Hour hand
- Moves 360° in 12 hours.
- Movement per hour = 30°.
- Movement per minute = 30° / 60 = 0.5°.

3.3 Relative movement
- The minute hand gains 5.5° on the hour hand every minute.
- Relative speed = 6° − 0.5° = 5.5° per minute.

4. ANGLE BETWEEN HANDS
4.1 Angle formula
Angle between hour and minute hand = |30H − (11/2)M|.
Here H is hour on 12-hour clock and M is minutes.

4.2 Alternate formula
Smaller angle = |30H − 5.5M|.
If value > 180°, subtract from 360° to get the acute angle.

4.3 Examples
- 12:00 → 0°
- 3:00 → 90°
- 6:00 → 180°
- 9:00 → 90° (because |270| becomes 90° by subtracting from 360°)

4.4 Solving practice
Example: Angle at 2:20.
Angle = |30×2 − 5.5×20| = |60 − 110| = 50°.

5. HANDS COINCIDENCE AND OPPOSITION
5.1 Coincidence (overlap)
The hands overlap every 65 5/11 minutes, or 12 times in 12 hours.
General formula for coincidence: Time = 65 5/11 × k minutes after 12:00, where k = 0, 1, 2, ... 11.

5.2 When hands are opposite
The hands are opposite (180° apart) 11 times in 12 hours.
General formula: Time after 12:00 = (360/11) × k minutes for k = 0, 1, 2, ... 10.

5.3 Finding coincidence time
To find time when hands coincide after H hours:
Time in minutes = 60H / 11.
Example: after 3 o’clock, coincidence occurs at 3:16 4/11.

5.4 Finding opposite time
Time in minutes = 60H / 11 + 30/11.
Example: after 3 o’clock, opposite occurs at 3:16 4/11 + 30/11 = 3:49 1/11.

6. SPECIFIC CLOCK PROBLEMS
6.1 Finding exact minute for a given angle
Solve |30H − 5.5M| = θ and simplify for M.

6.2 Hands in straight line
- Straight line can be 0° or 180°.
- 0° means overlap.
- 180° means opposite direction.

6.3 Hands forming a right angle
Set |30H − 5.5M| = 90° or 270° and solve.
There are 22 right-angle instances in 12 hours.

7. MIRROR IMAGE CLOCKS
7.1 Mirror time formula
Mirror time = 11:60 − original time (or 11:59 − original time, depending on convention).
Example: Mirror image of 3:20 is 8:40.

7.2 Understanding mirror time
- A clock reflected in a vertical mirror appears reversed left-to-right.
- The hour hand moves to the symmetric position.
- The minute hand also reverses.

8. CLOCK GAIN AND LOSS
8.1 Definitions
- Fast clock: gains time, shows more time than actual.
- Slow clock: loses time, shows less time than actual.

8.2 Finding actual time
If a clock gains x minutes in y hours, true time passage for 60 minutes shown = 60 × y / (y + x).
If a clock loses x minutes in y hours, true time passage for 60 minutes shown = 60 × y / (y − x).

8.3 Examples
If a clock gains 5 minutes in 3 hours, then when it shows 12:00 noon, actual time = 12:00 − 5 × 60 / (180 + 5) minutes.

8.4 Common gain/loss problems
- Find the time when a fast clock reads a certain time.
- Find the actual time when a slow clock reads a certain time.

9. ADDITIONAL CLOCK TOPICS
9.1 Angle covered by hands in a given time
- Minute hand covers 6° per minute.
- Hour hand covers 0.5° per minute.

9.2 Time when minute and hour hands swap positions
Solve using equations for both hand positions.

9.3 Time between two events
Use relative speed or direct angle formulas.

10. TIPS FOR SOLVING CLOCK PROBLEMS
- Convert the hour to 12-hour format first.
- Use the smaller angle when asked for the angle between hands.
- For exact times, work with fractions of minutes.
- Check whether the problem asks for true time, mirror time, overlap time, or opposite time.
- Avoid assuming the hands move only at integer minute marks.

11. PRACTICE QUESTIONS
1. What is the angle between hands at 5:30?
Answer: |150 − 165| = 15°.

2. At what time between 2 and 3 o’clock are the hands aligned?
Answer: 2:10 10/11.

3. What is the mirror image of 7:15?
Answer: 4:45.

4. A clock gains 10 minutes every 12 hours. If it reads 3:00 pm, what is the true time?
Answer: 2:50.

12. QUICK SUMMARY
- Minute hand moves 6° per minute.
- Hour hand moves 0.5° per minute.
- Angle = |30H − 5.5M|.
- Hands coincide every 65 5/11 minutes.
- Mirror time = 11:60 − original time.
- Fast clock gains time, slow clock loses time.

Clock problems are best solved by converting time into angles and using the correct relationship between hour and minute hands. Practice all types of questions to become comfortable with both exact times and angle-based reasoning.

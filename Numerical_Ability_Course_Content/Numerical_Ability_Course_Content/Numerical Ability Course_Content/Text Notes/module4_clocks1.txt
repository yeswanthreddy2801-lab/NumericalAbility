CLOCKS (TEXT NOTES)

============================================================

1. INTRODUCTION

A clock is a device used to measure time.
Clock problems in aptitude are based on angles, hands movement, mirror images, and clock errors.

============================================================

2. BASIC CONCEPTS

Clock has 2 main hands:
• Hour hand
• Minute hand

A full clock circle = 360°

============================================================

3. MOVEMENT OF HANDS

Minute Hand:
• Moves 360° in 60 minutes
• Moves 6° per minute

Hour Hand:
• Moves 360° in 12 hours
• Moves 30° per hour
• Moves 0.5° per minute

============================================================

4. ANGLE FORMULA

Angle between hour and minute hand:

Angle = |30H - (11/2)M|

Where:
H = hour
M = minutes

============================================================

5. IMPORTANT CLOCK ANGLES

• 12:00 → 0°
• 3:00 → 90°
• 6:00 → 180°
• 9:00 → 270°

============================================================

6. FULL ROTATION

• One full rotation = 360°
• Half rotation = 180°

============================================================

7. CLOCK RANK MOVEMENT

Minute hand:
• 30 minutes = 180°
• 15 minutes = 90°

Hour hand:
• 2 hours = 60°
• 1 hour = 30°

============================================================

8. MIRROR CLOCK CONCEPT

Mirror time formula:
Mirror Time = 11:60 - Original Time

Used to find reflected clock images.

============================================================

9. CLOCK ERROR / GAIN & LOSS

Clock may run fast or slow:
• Fast clock → gains time
• Slow clock → loses time

Error = difference between actual time and shown time

============================================================

10. COMMON TYPES OF QUESTIONS

• Angle between hands
• Mirror image of time
• Time when hands coincide
• Clock gain/loss problems
• Finding time after rotation

============================================================

11. HANDS COINCIDENCE

Hands coincide approximately every:
• 65 5/11 minutes

============================================================

12. IMPORTANT POINTS

• Minute hand is faster than hour hand
• Both hands overlap multiple times a day
• Use angle formula for speed solving

============================================================

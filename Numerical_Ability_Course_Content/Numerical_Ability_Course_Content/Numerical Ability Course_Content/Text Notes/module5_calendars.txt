CALENDARS (TEXT NOTES)

============================================================

1. INTRODUCTION

Calendar problems are based on finding days, dates, leap years, odd days, and weekday calculations.
These are commonly asked in aptitude exams like SSC, Banking, UPSC, and placement tests.

============================================================

2. TYPES OF YEARS

Ordinary Year:
• 365 days
• 52 weeks + 1 odd day

Leap Year:
• 366 days
• 52 weeks + 2 odd days

============================================================

3. LEAP YEAR RULES

A year is a leap year if:
• Divisible by 4 → Yes
• Divisible by 100 → Not leap year
• Divisible by 400 → Yes (exception)

Examples:
• 2000 → Leap Year
• 1900 → Not Leap Year
• 2024 → Leap Year
• 2025 → Not Leap Year

============================================================

4. MONTH DAYS

January → 31
February → 28 (29 in leap year)
March → 31
April → 30
May → 31
June → 30
July → 31
August → 31
September → 30
October → 31
November → 30
December → 31

============================================================

5. ODD DAYS

Odd days = extra days beyond complete weeks

• 1 week = 0 odd days
• 1 day = 1 odd day
• 365 days = 1 odd day
• 366 days = 2 odd days

============================================================

6. WEEKDAY MAPPING

0 → Sunday
1 → Monday
2 → Tuesday
3 → Wednesday
4 → Thursday
5 → Friday
6 → Saturday

============================================================

7. BASIC METHOD

Steps:
1. Calculate total days
2. Divide by 7
3. Take remainder (odd days)
4. Match with weekday mapping

============================================================

8. LEAP YEAR IMPACT

Leap years add 1 extra day in February.
This changes weekday calculations after February.

============================================================

9. CALENDAR REPEATING CYCLE

Calendar repeats every:
• 400 years

Because odd days reset after 400 years.

============================================================

10. IMPORTANT SHORTCUTS

• Memorize odd days for 100, 200, 300, 400 years
• Always check leap year first
• Use modulo 7 for quick calculation
• Learn month day pattern

============================================================

11. COMMON QUESTION TYPES

• Find day of a given date
• Check leap year
• Find odd days
• Calendar repetition problems
• Next/previous day calculation

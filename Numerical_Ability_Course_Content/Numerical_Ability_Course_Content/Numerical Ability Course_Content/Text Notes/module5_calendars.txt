CALENDARS (DETAILED NOTES)

============================================================
1. INTRODUCTION

Calendar problems ask for the weekday of a date, leap-year checks, odd days counting, and patterns in repeating calendars. This note explains concepts, gives multiple methods (odd-days, anchor-day, Zeller), step-by-step examples, and practice problems with answers.

============================================================
2. BASIC CONCEPTS

- Ordinary year = 365 days = 52 weeks + 1 odd day.
- Leap year = 366 days = 52 weeks + 2 odd days.
- Odd days = days beyond complete weeks; used to shift weekdays.
- Calendar repeats every 400 years (Gregorian cycle).

============================================================
3. LEAP YEAR RULE (GREGORIAN)

- If year % 400 == 0 → leap year.
- Else if year % 100 == 0 → not leap year.
- Else if year % 4 == 0 → leap year.
- Else → not leap year.

Examples: 2000 leap, 1900 not leap, 2024 leap.

Practical note: For day calculations, treat years modulo 400.

============================================================
4. ODD DAYS: QUICK TABLE

- 1 ordinary year → 1 odd day
- 1 leap year → 2 odd days
- 100 years → 5 odd days (because 100 years = 36524 days = 5203 weeks + 5)
- 200 years → 3 odd days
- 300 years → 1 odd day
- 400 years → 0 odd days (calendar resets)

Use these to speed up multi-year calculations.

============================================================
5. METHOD A — ODD DAYS METHOD (STEP-BY-STEP)

To find weekday for DD/MM/YYYY:
1. Count odd days contributed by complete years before YYYY (use cycles of 400, then 100s, then remaining years).
2. Add odd days contributed by months in the current year before the given month (use month table; account for leap year if month > February).
3. Add the day (DD) modulo 7.
4. Sum all odd days and reduce mod 7; map 0→Sunday,1→Monday,...6→Saturday.

Month odd-days table (non-leap):
- Jan 31 → 3 odd days
- Feb 28 → 0
- Mar 31 → 3
- Apr 30 → 2
- May 31 → 3
- Jun 30 → 2
- Jul 31 → 3
- Aug 31 → 3
- Sep 30 → 2
- Oct 31 → 3
- Nov 30 → 2
- Dec 31 → 3

For leap years, add 1 extra odd day for months after February.

Example: Day of week for 15 Aug 1947
- Years before 1947 = 1946 → split: 1600 (400*4) leaves 346. Use odd-days for 300/200/100 etc or compute directly: odd days for 1946 = (1946 % 400 calculation) → (quick approach below). Simpler worked example in practice section.

============================================================
6. METHOD B — DOOMSDAY / ANCHOR DAY (CONCISE)

Concept: Every year has a 'Doomsday' — a weekday that specific anchor dates fall on (e.g., 4/4, 6/6, 8/8, 10/10, 12/12). Once you know the doomsday for the year, compute the weekday of any date by offsetting from the nearest anchor date.

Steps (Conway's Doomsday algorithm simplified):
1. Break year YYYY into century part (C) and year part (y). For Gregorian: anchor day for centuries repeats every 400 years.
2. Compute: a = floor(y/12), b = y % 12, c = floor(b/4).
3. Sum s = a + b + c. Add century anchor offset and reduce mod 7 → doomsday.
4. Use nearest doomsday date for the month and adjust by day difference.

This method is fast with practice and requires memorizing anchor days for centuries (e.g., 2000s anchor = Tuesday, depending on mapping used).

============================================================
7. METHOD C — ZELLER'S CONGRUENCE (FORMULA)

Zeller's gives weekday directly (0=Saturday,...6=Friday). For Gregorian calendar:

h = (q + floor((13(m+1))/5) + K + floor(K/4) + floor(J/4) + 5J) mod 7

Where:
- q = day of month
- m = month (3=March,...,14=February; months Jan/Feb counted as 13/14 of previous year)
- K = year % 100 (adjusted if Jan/Feb)
- J = floor(year / 100)

Then map h to weekday. Zeller is precise but algebra-heavy; use when formula work is acceptable.

============================================================
8. COMMON EXAMPLES (WORKED)

Example 1 — 15 August 1947 (India independence)
Method: Odd-days shortcut (concise):
- Years before 1947 → 1946 years.
- Break into 400-year cycles: 1946 = 4×400 + 346 → 346 years remaining.
- Odd days for 300 years = 1, for 40 years = (40 years contain 10 leap years? better: use table) -- for clarity, use direct computation: number of leap years = floor(1946/4) − floor(1946/100) + floor(1946/400) = 486 − 19 + 4 = 471.
- Total days = 1946×365 + 471 → odd days = (1946 + 471) mod 7 = 2417 mod 7 = 2417 − 7×345 = 2417 − 2415 = 2 odd days.
- Months before Aug in 1947 (non-leap): Jan→3, Feb→0, Mar→3, Apr→2, May→3, Jun→2, Jul→3 = total 16 odd days → 16 mod7 = 2.
- Day = 15 → 15 mod7 = 1.
- Sum odd days = years(2) + months(2) + day(1) = 5 → 5→ Friday.

Answer: Friday.

Example 2 — 1 Jan 2000
- Years before 2000 = 1999. Leap calc: floor(1999/4) − floor(1999/100) + floor(1999/400) = 499 − 19 + 4 = 484.
- Years odd days = (1999 + 484) mod7 = 2483 mod7 = 2483 − 7×354 = 2483 − 2478 = 5.
- Months before Jan = 0; Day = 1 → 1 mod7 = 1.
- Sum = 6 → Saturday.

Answer: Saturday.

9. PRACTICE PROBLEMS (WITH ANSWERS)

1) What day was 26 January 1950?  Ans: Thursday.
2) What day will 1 January 2100 fall on? Ans: Friday (remember 2100 is not leap).
3) What day was 29 February 2000? Ans: Tuesday.
4) Find weekday of 31 Dec 1999. Ans: Friday.
5) Day of 4 July 1776 (US Independence). Ans: Thursday (Julian/Gregorian caveats historically apply).

============================================================
10. TIPS & SHORTCUTS

- Use odd-days tables for quick manual calculation.
- For many questions, compute years' odd days by (years + leap_years) mod7.
- Keep month odd-days and adjust for leap year after February.
- Use Doomsday for fast mental calculations once anchors are memorized.
- For competitive exams, practice converting fractional steps to mod7 quickly.

============================================================
11. COMMON PITFALLS

- Forgetting to treat Jan/Feb as previous year in Zeller's algorithm.
- Ignoring century and 400-year exceptions for leap years.
- Mixing up mapping of numeric result to weekday ordering.

============================================================
SUMMARY

- Count odd days from years, months and date; reduce mod7.
- Remember leap-year rules and 400-year cycle.
- Use Doomsday or Zeller for faster computation if comfortable.

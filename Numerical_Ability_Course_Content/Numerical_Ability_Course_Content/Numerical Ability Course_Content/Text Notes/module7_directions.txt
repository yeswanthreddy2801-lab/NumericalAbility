============================================================
DIRECTIONS - FLASH CARDS (BASIC CONCEPTS)
============================================================

1. BASIC DIRECTIONS

North (N) → Up
South (S) → Down
East (E) → Right
West (W) → Left

------------------------------------------------------------

2. TURNING RULES

Right Turn → Clockwise direction
Left Turn  → Anti-clockwise direction

------------------------------------------------------------

3. ANGLE KNOWLEDGE (IMPORTANT)

Right Angle Turn → 90°
Straight → 0°
U-turn → 180°

------------------------------------------------------------

4. RELATIVE DIRECTIONS

North-East (NE) → Between North and East
North-West (NW) → Between North and West
South-East (SE) → Between South and East
South-West (SW) → Between South and West

------------------------------------------------------------

5. COMMON QUESTION TYPES

• Final direction after multiple turns
• Shortest distance problems
• Starting and ending point problems
• Movement in grid/maze
• Clockwise/anti-clockwise rotation

------------------------------------------------------------

6. SHORTCUT RULES

• Always draw diagram first
• Convert all directions to N, S, E, W
• Use arrow method for movements
• Track final position step by step
• Cancel opposite movements (N vs S, E vs W)

------------------------------------------------------------

7. IMPORTANT CONCEPTS

Opposite directions:
North ↔ South
East ↔ West

Net movement = Final displacement after cancelling opposite moves

------------------------------------------------------------

8. SAMPLE QUICK CASE

Q: A person moves North → East → South → West

Result: Back to starting point (0 displacement)

------------------------------------------------------------

9. COMMON MISTAKES

• Mixing left/right with east/west
• Not drawing diagrams
• Forgetting rotation direction
• Skipping intermediate steps

------------------------------------------------------------

10. REAL LIFE USES

• Navigation systems (GPS)
• Map reading
• Robotics movement
• Games (maze solving)
• Airport/railway navigation

============================================================
RANKINGS & POSITIONS (DETAILED NOTES)
============================================================

1. OVERVIEW

Ranking problems ask for positions (ranks) of persons/objects in a line, circle, or other arrangement. Common operations: find total number, convert between left/right ranks, insert/remove people, and handle ties or circular arrangements.

============================================================
2. SIMPLE LINEAR RANKING (KEY FORMULA)

If a person is Lth from left and Rth from right in a single line, then:

Total = L + R − 1

Reason: counting the person once avoids double-counting.

Example: L=4, R=6 ⇒ Total = 4+6−1 = 9.

============================================================
3. FINDING POSITION FROM OPPOSITE SIDE

- If person is kth from left, position from right = Total − k + 1.
- If person is mth from right, position from left = Total − m + 1.

Example: Total 20, position 7th from left → from right = 20 − 7 + 1 = 14.

============================================================
4. INSERTIONS AND DELETIONS

- When x people are inserted in front of a person, their left-rank increases by x.
- When people are removed before a person, adjust rank accordingly.

Example: A is 5th from left. If 3 people join at the left end, A becomes 8th from left.

============================================================
5. WORKING WITH TWO OR MORE GIVEN RELATIONS

Often problems give relative ranks for multiple people; set up equations using Total (N) and solve.

Example:
- "A is 3rd from left. B is 4th from right. C is exactly in the middle." Use formulas to relate positions and find N.

Technique: express each person's position in terms of N and set equalities if relations between them are given.

============================================================
6. CIRCULAR RANKING

- In circular arrangements, "from left/right" doesn't apply; use clockwise/counterclockwise positions.
- Key fact: in a circle, only relative order matters; there is no fixed first position unless specified.

============================================================
7. HANDLING TIES & EQUAL RANKS

- If two persons tie at the same rank, subsequent ranks may be skipped depending on convention (dense vs standard ranking). In typical placement puzzles ties are rare — clarify problem statement.

============================================================
8. COMMON TRICKS & SHORTCUTS

- Use the Total = L + R − 1 formula whenever left and right ranks are directly known.
- For pairwise relations, convert all ranks to expressions with N (total) and solve linear equations.
- When given multiple people positions relative to each other, draw the line and place fixed anchors.

============================================================
9. WORKED EXAMPLES

a) Example: "A is 7th from left and B is 5th from right. If B is just left of A, find total persons."
- Let A position from left = 7 ⇒ A index = 7. B is just left of A ⇒ B index from left = 6.
- B is 5th from right ⇒ Total N satisfies N − 5 + 1 = 6 ⇒ N − 4 = 6 ⇒ N = 10.

b) Example: "C is 3rd from left, D is 4th from left, and C is 5 places ahead of D from right." Solve for N.
- Express C's right position = N − 3 + 1 = N − 2.
- D's right position = N − 4 + 1 = N − 3.
- If C is 5 places ahead of D from right: (N − 3) − (N − 2) = 5 ⇒ −1 = 5 (contradiction) — re-check interpretation: likely meaning C is 5 places ahead of D when counting from right, meaning (D_right − C_right) = 5 ⇒ (N−3) − (N−2) = 5 ⇒ −1 = 5 — impossible; problem likely mis-stated. Always validate problem wording.

c) Example: "Aaron is 12th from left and 9th from right. How many people?" N = 12 + 9 − 1 = 20.

============================================================
10. PRACTICE PROBLEMS (ANSWERS FOLLOW)

1) P is 8th from left, Q is 6th from right. If Q is immediately right of P, find total.
2) R is 10th from left and 11th from right. How many people are there?
3) S is 7th from left. After insertion of 5 people at the left, S becomes 12th. Verify.
4) A and B stand in a row of N people. A is 4th from right, B is 6th from left. If B is 3 places to the left of A, find N.
5) In a circle, X is 3rd clockwise to Y. If there are 10 people, what's Y's position anticlockwise from X?

Answers:
1) Let P index = p, Q index = p+1 ⇒ from left p=8 ⇒ Q left index=9. Q right =6 ⇒ N − 6 +1 =9 ⇒ N−5=9 ⇒ N=14.
2) N = 10 + 11 − 1 = 20.
3) Insertion at left increases rank by 5: new rank = 7 + 5 = 12 (verified).
4) Let B left index = 6, A left index = 6+3 = 9. A right = 4 ⇒ N − 4 + 1 = 9 ⇒ N − 3 = 9 ⇒ N = 12.
5) In a circle of 10, anticlockwise distance from X to Y is 3 (mirror direction), so Y is 3 anticlockwise positions from X.

============================================================
11. COMMON ERRORS & VERIFICATION

- Error: forgetting to subtract 1 when converting L and R to total.
- Error: misplacing "just left" vs "immediately right" — draw a sketch.
- Verification: after computing N, check all given positions fit within 1..N and relationships hold.

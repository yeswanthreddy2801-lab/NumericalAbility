MAN-CHAIN RULE AND WAGES

## Introduction

Man-Chain Rule and Wages is an advanced application of Time and Work concepts. It is widely asked in placement exams to test proportional reasoning and efficiency-based calculations.

This topic deals with:
- Number of workers (men, women, or machines)
- Number of days
- Hours worked per day
- Amount of work or wages

The key idea is:

Work is directly proportional to:
- Number of workers
- Number of days
- Hours per day

---

## Basic Formula

M1 × D1 × H1 × W1 = M2 × D2 × H2 × W2

Where:
M = Men (or workers)
D = Days
H = Hours per day
W = Work done

---

## Core Principle

More workers → Less time  
Less workers → More time  

Work remains constant in balanced conditions.

---

## Direct Variation Rule

If:
- Men increase → Work increases
- Days increase → Work increases
- Hours increase → Work increases

---

## Inverse Variation Rule

If:
- Men increase → Time decreases
- Efficiency increases → Time decreases

---

## Shortcut Formula

M1 × D1 × W1 = M2 × D2 × W2

(Used when working hours are same)

---

## Wages Concept

Wages are distributed based on work done.

Wage ∝ Work

If:
A works more → A gets more wage

---

## Efficiency Concept

If:
A is twice as efficient as B

Then:
A does twice the work in same time

---

## Solved Example 1

10 men complete a work in 12 days.

How many days will 15 men take?

Solution:

M1 × D1 = M2 × D2

10 × 12 = 15 × D2

120 = 15D2

D2 = 8 days

Answer: 8 days

---

## Solved Example 2

8 men working 6 hours per day complete work in 15 days.

How many days will 12 men working 4 hours take?

Solution:

M1 × D1 × H1 = M2 × D2 × H2

8 × 15 × 6 = 12 × D2 × 4

720 = 48D2

D2 = 15 days

---

## Wages Example

A and B work together and earn 900.

A does 60% work.

B does 40% work.

A's wage = 540  
B's wage = 360

---

## Common Mistakes

❌ Mixing efficiency and manpower incorrectly  
❌ Ignoring hours factor  
❌ Assuming equal wages  
❌ Forgetting proportionality rules  

---

## Real World Applications

- Factory labor distribution
- Construction workforce planning
- Salary distribution
- Team project allocation
- Machine-based production

---

## Placement Tips

- Always use M × D × H rule
- Keep units consistent
- Convert everything into proportional form
- Avoid direct division of values
- Practice ratio-based questions

---

## Interview Questions

1. What is Man-Chain Rule?
It is a proportionality rule used in work-time problems involving men, days, and hours.

2. What is the main formula?
M × D × H ∝ Work

3. How are wages distributed?
Based on work done.

4. What happens if workers increase?
Time decreases.

5. Why is this topic important?
It is frequently asked in aptitude tests.

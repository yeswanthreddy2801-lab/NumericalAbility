MAN-CHAIN RULE, WAGES, AND EFFICIENCY - DETAILED NOTES

1. INTRODUCTION
Man-Chain Rule and Wages is an extension of Time and Work concepts. It combines worker count, working days, daily hours, efficiency, and wage distribution. This topic is common in placement exams and tests proportional reasoning.

2. CORE IDEA
Total work done is directly proportional to:
- Number of workers
- Number of days worked
- Hours worked per day
- Efficiency of each worker

If total work is constant, increasing one factor allows decreasing another.

3. BASIC FORMULAS
3.1 Work relationship
If work is constant:
M1 × D1 × H1 = M2 × D2 × H2
Where:
- M = number of workers
- D = number of days
- H = hours per day

3.2 When efficiency differs
Work = Number of workers × days × hours × individual efficiency
If all workers have same efficiency, the formula simplifies to:
M1 × D1 × H1 = M2 × D2 × H2

3.3 Wages and work
Wage is proportional to the amount of work done.
Wages shared in ratio of work done by each worker.

4. MAN-CHAIN RULE
The man-chain rule says that when work is fixed:
- More men means less time.
- Fewer men means more time.

If men and days are the only variables and hours remain constant:
M1 × D1 = M2 × D2
If men and hours vary:
M1 × D1 × H1 = M2 × D2 × H2

5. EFFICIENCY
Efficiency is the work done per unit time.
If A is k times as efficient as B,
- Ratio of efficiency = k : 1
- Ratio of times required = 1 : k
So if A does the work in 10 days, B does it in 10k days.

6. WORK, RATE, AND TIME
When total work is W:
Work = Rate × Time
Rate = Work / Time
Time = Work / Rate

If one worker can finish a job in N days, his one-day work = 1/N.
If two workers A and B work together, combined one-day work = 1/x + 1/y.

7. WAGES DISTRIBUTION
When workers share a wage based on work done:
Wage ratio = Work done ratio.
If A does p fraction of work and B does q fraction, then:
A’s share = total wage × p/(p+q)
B’s share = total wage × q/(p+q)

8. COMMON TYPES OF QUESTIONS
8.1 Same work different workers
If 10 men complete a job in 12 days, how long will 15 men take?
8.2 Different hours per day
If 8 men work 6 hours and complete a job in 15 days, how long will 12 men working 4 hours take?
8.3 Efficiency based questions
A is twice as efficient as B. If A takes 10 days, find B’s time.
8.4 Wages division
A and B complete a job together. A does 60% of work. Distribute total wage accordingly.

9. SOLVED EXAMPLES
Example 1: Same hours
10 men complete work in 12 days. How many days will 15 men take?
M1 × D1 = M2 × D2
10 × 12 = 15 × D2
120 = 15 × D2
D2 = 8 days

Example 2: Different hours
8 men work 6 hours a day and finish in 15 days. 12 men work 4 hours a day. Find time.
8 × 15 × 6 = 12 × D2 × 4
720 = 48 × D2
D2 = 15 days

Example 3: Efficiency relation
A is twice as efficient as B. If A takes 10 days, B takes 20 days.
A’s efficiency : B’s efficiency = 2 : 1.
Time ratio = 1 : 2.

Example 4: Wage sharing
A and B earn 900 together.
A does 60% of work and B does 40%.
A’s wage = 900 × 60/100 = 540.
B’s wage = 900 × 40/100 = 360.

Example 5: Combined work with different efficiency
A can do a job in 9 days, B in 12 days. How long together?
A’s one-day work = 1/9.
B’s one-day work = 1/12.
Combined = 1/9 + 1/12 = 4/36 + 3/36 = 7/36.
Time = 36/7 days.

10. TIPS AND SHORTCUTS
- Always convert the problem to rates first.
- Use M×D×H when hours are involved.
- Use M×D if hours remain constant.
- Use ratios for efficiency and wages.
- Check units carefully.
- Assume total work = LCM of days when helpful.
- If workers join or leave, calculate partial work before and after the change.

11. COMMON MISTAKES
- Mixing up men and wages.
- Ignoring the hours factor in work calculations.
- Applying M×D formula when hours differ.
- Dividing wages equally regardless of work done.
- Not converting efficiency into time ratio.

12. PRACTICE QUESTIONS
1. 12 men complete a job in 24 days. How long will 8 men take?
Answer: 36 days.

2. 6 men working 5 hours a day finish in 20 days. How many days will 10 men working 8 hours a day take?
Solution: 6 × 20 × 5 = 10 × D × 8 → D = 7.5 days.

3. A is 3 times as efficient as B. If A takes 12 days, how long will B take?
Answer: 36 days.

4. Total wage is 1000. A does 70% work, B does 30%. A’s share = 700, B’s share = 300.

13. APPLICATIONS
- Salary and bonus sharing
- Construction workforce planning
- Machine productivity optimization
- Project scheduling
- Outsourcing and labor allocation

14. QUICK SUMMARY
- Work ∝ Men × Days × Hours.
- Wages ∝ Work done.
- Efficiency × Time = constant for same work.
- More workers or more hours reduce time.
- If efficiency doubles, time halves.

TIME AND WORK - DETAILED NOTES

1. INTRODUCTION
Time and Work is a core aptitude topic used in competitive exams and placement tests. It models how people, machines, or teams complete tasks over time. The focus is on rates of work and combining them correctly.

2. FUNDAMENTAL RELATION
Work = Rate × Time

Where:
- Work is the total job to be completed.
- Rate is the fraction of work done per unit time.
- Time is the duration taken to complete the work.

If total work = 1 unit, then:
One day’s work = 1 / number of days.

3. BASIC TERMINOLOGY
- One day’s work: Portion of the task done in one day.
- Efficiency: Work done per day; directly proportional to rate.
- Time required: Total work divided by rate.

4. PRIMARY FORMULAS
Work = Rate × Time
Rate = Work / Time
Time = Work / Rate

When total work is assumed as 1 unit:
One day’s work = 1 / N if a worker finishes in N days.

5. COMBINED WORK
5.1 Two workers
If A finishes in x days and B finishes in y days:
A’s one-day work = 1/x
B’s one-day work = 1/y
Combined one-day work = 1/x + 1/y
Time together = 1 / (1/x + 1/y) = xy/(x+y)

5.2 Three workers
A’s rate = 1/x, B’s rate = 1/y, C’s rate = 1/z
Combined rate = 1/x + 1/y + 1/z
Time together = 1 / (1/x + 1/y + 1/z)

5.3 Work done in a given time
If rate = R, work done in t days = R × t.

6. EFFICIENCY AND TIME RELATION
Efficiency ∝ Work done per day.
Time ∝ 1 / Efficiency.

If A is twice as efficient as B:
Efficiency ratio A:B = 2:1
Time ratio A:B = 1:2

7. ADVANCED METHODS
7.1 Ratio method
If efficiencies are given as A:B = p:q,
Time ratio = q:p.

7.2 LCM method
Choose total work equal to the LCM of all given days. This simplifies fractional calculations.
Example:
A = 12 days, B = 18 days.
LCM = 36.
A’s work/day = 3 units.
B’s work/day = 2 units.
Combined = 5 units/day.
Time = 36/5 days.

7.3 Partial work
If a worker completes p portion in t days, rate = p / t.

7.4 Work with breaks or varying durations
Compute work separately for each time period and sum the contributions.

8. COMMON PROBLEMS
8.1 Two workers working together
Time = xy/(x+y)

8.2 Worker starts alone, then another joins
Work done by first worker until join + work done together for remaining work.

8.3 One worker leaves before completion
Total work = work done by each worker during their active periods.

8.4 Efficiency increases or decreases
If one worker is k times as efficient as another, use ratios to solve.

8.5 Work with machines and people
Treat every machine or person as a separate worker with its own rate.

9. SOLVED EXAMPLES
Example 1: Two people working together
A can complete a task in 10 days and B can complete it in 20 days. Time together?
A’s one-day work = 1/10.
B’s one-day work = 1/20.
Combined work = 1/10 + 1/20 = 3/20.
Time = 20/3 = 6.67 days.

Example 2: Efficiency relation
A can complete a job in 24 days. B is twice as efficient as A. How long for B?
Since B is twice as efficient, B’s time = 24/2 = 12 days.
Combined time = 1 / (1/24 + 1/12) = 8 days.

Example 3: Worker joins later
A completes a job in 18 days. After 6 days, B joins and B alone can complete the job in 9 days. When will the work finish?
A’s work in 6 days = 6 × 1/18 = 1/3.
Remaining work = 2/3.
Combined rate = 1/18 + 1/9 = 1/6.
Time to finish = (2/3) ÷ (1/6) = 4 days.
Total time = 10 days.

Example 4: Worker leaves early
A can do the job in 8 days, B in 12 days. They start together, and A leaves after 3 days. How long will B take more?
Combined rate = 1/8 + 1/12 = 5/24 per day.
Work done in 3 days = 3 × 5/24 = 5/8.
Remaining work = 3/8.
B’s rate = 1/12.
Additional time = (3/8) ÷ (1/12) = 4.5 days.
Total time = 7.5 days.

Example 5: Part of the work
A completes a task in 15 days. How long to finish 2/3 of the work?
Rate = 1/15.
Time = (2/3) ÷ (1/15) = 10 days.

10. SHORTCUTS AND TRICKS
- Assume total work = 1 unless a different value is useful.
- Convert all times to rates before adding.
- Use positive rates for work done and negative only for cancellation or removal problems.
- Keep units consistent (days, hours, minutes).
- Memorize xy/(x+y) as the formula for two workers.
- Use LCM for simpler fraction arithmetic.

11. COMMON MISTAKES
- Adding days instead of rates.
- Using the same unit for rates and time incorrectly.
- Forgetting that efficiency is inverse to time.
- Misreading “together” or “alone for some time.”
- Using different assumptions for total work in the same problem.

12. PRACTICE QUESTIONS
1. A can complete a job in 15 days and B in 20 days. How long together?
Answer: 60/7 days.

2. A does a job in 12 days, B in 18 days, C in 24 days. How long together?
Answer: 24/9 = 8/3 days.

3. A is twice as efficient as B. If A takes 10 days, how long does B take?
Answer: 20 days.

4. A and B together can finish a work in 8 days. B alone takes 12 days. How long will A alone take?
Answer: 24 days.

13. REAL-WORLD APPLICATIONS
- Construction and project completion schedules
- Manufacturing assembly lines
- Machine productivity planning
- Team-based task allocation
- Software development and testing workflows
- Resource utilization in factories

14. QUICK SUMMARY
- Work = Rate × Time.
- One day’s work = 1 / total days.
- Combine work rates, not days.
- Efficiency and time are inversely related.
- Use xy/(x+y) for two workers working together.
- Use LCM to simplify fraction-based work calculations.

Time and Work is a fundamental concept for reasoning-based aptitude. Practice a wide variety of problems, especially those with workers joining, leaving, or working at different efficiencies.

COMBINATIONS

## Introduction

Combination means selecting objects from a group where the order of selection does not matter.

If two selections contain the same objects, they are considered identical regardless of their order.

Example

Selecting A and B

is the same as

Selecting B and A

Therefore,

AB = BA

--------------------------------------------------

## Formula

nCr = n! / [r!(n−r)!]

where

n = total objects

r = objects selected

--------------------------------------------------

## When to Use Combination

Use Combination when:

• Selecting students

• Forming committees

• Choosing team members

• Selecting books

• Choosing interview candidates

--------------------------------------------------

## Combination Properties

nC0 = 1

nCn = 1

nC1 = n

nCr = nC(n−r)

--------------------------------------------------

## Solved Example 1

Choose 2 students from 5 students.

5C2

= 5! / (2!3!)

= 120 / (2×6)

= 10

Answer:

10 ways

--------------------------------------------------

## Solved Example 2

Choose 3 books from 8 books.

8C3

= 8! / (3!5!)

= (8×7×6)/(3×2×1)

= 56

--------------------------------------------------

## Solved Example 3

Choose a committee of 4 members from 10 people.

10C4

= 210

--------------------------------------------------

## Shortcut Techniques

• Use

nCr = nC(n−r)

Choose the smaller value of r.

Example

12C10

=

12C2

which is much easier.

• Cancel factorials before multiplying.

• Never calculate huge factorials completely.

--------------------------------------------------

## Difference from Permutation

Permutation

• Arrangement

• Order matters

Combination

• Selection

• Order does not matter

--------------------------------------------------

## Common Mistakes

• Using permutation formula

• Ignoring symmetry property

• Calculating full factorial unnecessarily

• Treating selection as arrangement

## Interview Questions

1. What is Combination?

Answer:
Selection where order does not matter.

2. Formula of Combination?

Answer:

nCr = n! / [r!(n−r)!]

3. Difference between Combination and Permutation?

Answer:
Combination is selection.
Permutation is arrangement.

4. What is nC0?

Answer:
1

5. Why is nCr = nC(n−r)?

Answer:
Choosing r objects is equivalent to rejecting (n−r) objects.

--------------------------------------------------

## Quick Summary

• Combination = Selection

• Order does NOT matter

• Formula

nCr = n! / [r!(n−r)!]

• nCr = nC(n−r)

• Always simplify factorials before calculation
